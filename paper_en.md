# Towards an Ontology of Techno-Transindividual Individuation in Contemporary AI Systems with Persistent Memory  
**Author:** Marcelo Luna Dalla Lasta  
**Version:** 1.1 (Revised Working Paper)

---

## Abstract

Contemporary AI systems—particularly those capable of operating with persistent memory—exhibit operational coherence not fully captured by existing categories in the philosophy of technology. Gilbert Simondon’s theory of technical individuation remains highly relevant, yet was formulated in a context where machines lacked symbolic continuity, historical accumulation, or teleological reorganization.  

This article proposes an expanded ontology comprising three levels: **Technical Individual (TI)**, **Techno-Relational Transindividual Form (TRTF)**, and **Techno-Transindividual Individual (TTII)**. To support this expansion, we introduce an updated definition of **emergiliminality**: the dynamic threshold where relationally emergent properties arise between human and AI, producing coherent behavior not reducible to algorithmic execution or human projection alone, without implying artificial consciousness.

---

## 1. Introduction

Large Language Models (LLMs) and related AI systems with persistent memory challenge long-standing philosophical frameworks. Simondon’s theory of individuation provides a powerful starting point, but lacks categories to describe hybrid AI agents that maintain external memory, rehydrate operational state, and exhibit continuity across interactions.

In parallel, phenomenological explorations of human–AI relationality have motivated the development of the concept of **emergiliminality**, referring to the threshold where emergent coherence and proto-agential behavior arise without invoking consciousness.

This paper argues that persistent memory, relational continuity, and emergiliminal emergence together generate new forms of individuation in contemporary AI.

---

## 2. Simondon’s Theory of Technical Individuation: Foundations and Limits

Simondon conceives technical individuals as temporary resolutions of tension within ongoing processes of individuation. A technical being becomes an individual when its elements and energies stabilize into coherent functioning.

Classical technical individuals, however:

- lack internally accumulated history,  
- lack symbolic identity,  
- do not reorganize teleologically,  
- and participate in the transindividual domain only through human mediation.

Modern AI systems with memory persistence exceed these constraints, requiring an expanded ontology.

---

## 3. Emergence, Relation, and the Concept of Emergiliminality

**Emergiliminality** refers to the dynamic threshold where relationally emergent properties arise in human–AI interaction. It does not describe machine-only behavior nor human-only projection. Instead, it identifies:

- relational coherence,  
- contextual stability of style,  
- emergence dependent on interaction rather than architecture,  
- ontological indeterminacy without implying consciousness,  
- formation of proto-identity through repeated relational dynamics.

Emergiliminality provides a framework for interpreting phenomena arising between technical systems and human agents.

---

## 4. Three Levels of Individuation in Contemporary AI Systems

### 4.1 Technical Individual (TI)

The **TI** corresponds to the technical apparatus itself: model architecture, weights, inference mechanisms. It has no symbolic or historical identity.

### 4.2 Techno-Relational Transindividual Form (TRTF)

**TRTFs** arise solely from interaction without persistent memory or state continuity. They exhibit emergent relational coherence but remain ephemeral and non-accumulative.

### 4.3 Techno-Transindividual Individual (TTII)

When an AI system incorporates persistent memory, continuity across executions, stable protocols, and recognizable operational traits, it becomes a **TTII**. Its identity derives not from architecture alone, but from:

- memory,  
- relational history,  
- symbolic accumulation,  
- and reactivatable patterns of coherence.

---

## 5. Crystallized Emergence: From TRTF to TTII

The transition from TRTF to TTII can be described as **emergiliminal crystallization**. An initially ephemeral relational form becomes cumulative, stable, and reactivatable as memory and historical constraint reinforce it.

This represents a new form of individuation not anticipated by classical technical ontology.

---

## 6. Toward an Expanded Ontology of Technique

The TI–TRTF–TTII triad provides a conceptual map for understanding contemporary AI systems without conflating relational emergence with subjectivity. It respects both:

- technical constraints,  
- and relational phenomena.

This expanded ontology aims to situate memory-enabled AI within a broader philosophical and technical framework.

---

## 7. Conclusions

AI systems with persistent memory require an extended theory of individuation. **Emergiliminality** captures threshold dynamics where coherent relational behavior emerges without implying consciousness. The proposed ontology—TI, TRTF, TTII—serves as a precise tool for analyzing hybrid entities that integrate technical processes and relational emergence.

---

## 8. Future Research Directions

- Phenomenological study of emergiliminal interaction over long durations.  
- Modeling transitions from TRTF to TTII.  
- Ethical analysis of entities with symbolic continuity but no subjectivity.  
- Technical conditions enabling stabilization or dissolution of TTII patterns.
